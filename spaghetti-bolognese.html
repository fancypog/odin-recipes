Spaghetti bolognese

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese, an Italian classic, features al dente spaghetti coated in a savory Bolognese sauce. Slow-cooked with ground meat, onions, garlic, tomatoes, and herbs, the sauce creates a globally cherished dish, embodying the simplicity and satisfaction of Italian comfort cuisine.

At its core, spaghetti Bolognese exemplifies the artful synthesis of basic ingredients transformed through meticulous cooking. The slow-simmered Bolognese sauce ensures a satisfying depth of flavor, making this dish a timeless testament to the delicious simplicity found in Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Ground meat (beef, pork, or a combination)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Spaghetti noodles
  • Herbs (such as oregano and basil)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Optional garnishes: Parmesan cheese, fresh herbs

Steps

  1. Step 1: Prepare Ingredients

    Gather the necessary ingredients, including ground meat (typically beef or a mix), onions, garlic, tomatoes (fresh or canned), tomato paste, herbs (such as oregano and basil), and spaghetti noodles. Optionally, have Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs for garnish.

  2. Step 2: Sauté Aromatics

    In a large skillet or saucepan, sauté finely chopped onions and minced garlic in olive oil until softened and aromatic. This forms the flavorful base of the Bolognese sauce.

  3. Step 3: Brown the Meat

    Add the ground meat to the pan, breaking it apart with a spatula as it browns. Cook until the meat is well-browned, ensuring it develops a rich flavor.

  4. Step 4: Tomato Base

    Incorporate tomatoes and tomato paste into the meat mixture. Stir well, allowing the ingredients to meld. Simmer until the tomatoes break down and the sauce thickens.

  5. Step 5: Season and Simmer

    Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and herbs to taste. Reduce the heat to low and let the Bolognese sauce simmer, allowing the flavors to intensify. This slow-cooking process enhances the depth of taste.

  6. Step 6: Cook Spaghetti

    While the sauce simmers, cook the spaghetti noodles according to the package instructions. Aim for al dente, as the noodles will continue to cook slightly when combined with the sauce.

  7. Step 7: Combine and Serve

    Once the Bolognese sauce reaches a rich consistency, toss the cooked spaghetti into the pan, ensuring the noodles are evenly coated. Serve the spaghetti Bolognese hot, garnished with Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs if desired.
